Special Political and Decolonization Committee

Committee Description

Topic A: Foreign Military Bases in Africa

In the vast continent of Africa, foreign military bases stand as stark symbols of external influence and control. The very presence of these bases can stir feelings of resentment, perpetuating a narrative of continued foreign intervention in Africa's affairs. The potential for foreign military bases to exacerbate existing conflicts and fuel regional tensions cannot be ignored. Concentrations of military power in particular areas can unsettle the delicate balance of power, leading to increased militarization and the potential for proxy wars. This issue evokes a complex web of power dynamics, strategic interests, and the ever-present shadow of neocolonialism. This issue cannot be ignored, and so as delegates of SPECPOL, it is your job to address this issue.

Topic B: The Myanmar Humanitarian Crisis

The ongoing crisis in Myanmar has garnered significant international attention due to its complex nature and the far-reaching implications it holds for the country and its people. This crisis emerged from a confluence of political, ethnic, and social factors, which have contributed to a volatile and challenging situation for Myanmar's population. Emerging from this crisis are humanitarian problems. The plight of internally displaced persons, refugees, and affected communities must be addressed through the provision of humanitarian aid, protection, and support. SPECPOL must strive to work a way to manage this humanitarian crisis in Myanmar that is not only affetcing its people, but also nations beyond.
